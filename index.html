Mumps cases in central Ohio

The first case of mumps detected in Franklin County was January 7, with the first case at OSU detected February 10.1

At Ohio State University, the virus has affected 108 students, 15 staff members, two family members, and 24 with strong ties to the university community, with most affected OSU-affiliated persons living in Columbus. 91 females and 58 males are affected at OSU, from three to 58 years of age. The university-linked cases include residents in Columbus, Athens, Belmont, Delaware, Fairfield, Franklin, Hamilton, Licking, and Pickaway counties.2

Two cases have been reported in Olentangy Local Schools, leading school officials to tell students without the vaccine and with compromised immune systems to stay home. 3
